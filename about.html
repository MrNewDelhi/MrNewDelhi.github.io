About.

Anmol · QA Engineer · 2.5 years · Delhi, India. Open to QA / SDET / AI-QA-Engineer roles at AI-first companies.

Now

QA Engineer at Lotusflare Technologies, working on Smartless Mobile — a T-Mobile US MVNE on a cloud-native, CMS-driven e-commerce stack. I focus on AI-augmented test infrastructure: agents on MCP, LLM evaluation, multimodal ticket triage, deterministic smoke testing across pre-prod and prod.

Before

Automation Test Engineer at L&T Technology Services on the T-Mobile US program. Led a 6-engineer QA pod across iOS, Android, and Web for two MVNE clients: Smartless Mobile and Roccstar Wireless. Built and shipped a multi-model self-healing Appium framework that ran in production, later centralized behind a Flask + SQLite service so the locator cache and telemetry were shared across the pod.

OSS → BSS

My two stops trace the telecom stack end-to-end. At L&T Technology Services I started on the OSS side — network testing for the T-Mobile program — and contributed to the architecture of an internal proprietary platform (IA tool) that combined a device farm with a network-testing harness. Alongside it I drove cross-vendor coverage with Perfecto, Katalon Studio, Applitools, and an in-house Appium device farm.

The role bled into infra: I stood up Citrix XenDesktop servers, opened up remote access through FortiGate firewalls, and helped land the T-Mobile client lab for network plus UI/API testing. The IT-adjacent work was where I learned that a reliable test signal is mostly a reliable lab.

At Lotusflare I moved to the BSS side — commerce, catalog, ordering, and the cloud-native CMS layer that sits in front of the network. Same customer (T-Mobile US, via Smartless Mobile), opposite end of the stack. Owning both sides is what makes the end-to-end story coherent for me: I have seen the packet path and the checkout funnel for the same SIM.

How I work

  • I default to building tools over heroics. If the same toil shows up twice, I write the thing that kills it.
  • I write things down. The 1,658-line test case writing guide that powers the AIO TCC skill started as a Notion doc.
  • I sit in the bug triage. I sit in the PM standup. QA is a cross-team function and the lift comes from sitting in the right rooms.
  • I prefer deterministic over clever. A boring suite that runs the same way every time beats a magical one that fails on Tuesdays.

Outside of work
